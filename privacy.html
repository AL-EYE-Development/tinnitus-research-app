Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 2026

Study Title: Investigation of Tinnitus and Related Symptoms

1. Introduction

This privacy policy applies to the DTU Tinnitus Study application. The purpose of this academic study is to investigate the relationship between tinnitus and various related symptoms, including how tinnitus is perceived and affects daily life.

The data controller for this application is the Technical University of Denmark (DTU) - Hearing Systems Section (DTU Health Tech).

2. What Information We Collect

To conduct this research, the application collects the following information directly provided by you:

  • Demographic Data: Age, sex, and handedness.
  • Tinnitus Characteristics: Onset, duration, pitch, perceived loudness, and the impact of the condition on your daily life, concentration, and sleep.
  • Health and Medical History: History of hearing problems, ear infections, dizziness, visual static, pain syndromes, and previously diagnosed medical conditions (e.g., neurological, cardiovascular, or psychological conditions).
  • Tinnitus Matching Results: Data generated from the in-app audio matching test (frequency, sound type, pan, volume, and pulse speed/depth).

The app does not collect background location, advertising identifiers, or background audio recordings.

3. How We Use Your Information

Your data will be treated strictly confidentially and used solely for academic research purposes. The information helps our research team map standardized profiles for tinnitus and related auditory symptoms.

4. Data Storage and Security

All data collected through this application is securely transmitted and stored in a database that only the Technical University of Denmark (DTU) has access to. No personal information that could identify you will be published. When research findings are published, all data will be aggregated and fully anonymized.

5. Your Rights and Withdrawal of Consent

Your participation is completely voluntary. Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), you have the right to:

  • Access the data we hold about you.
  • Withdraw your consent at any time without giving a reason and without any negative consequences.
  • Request the deletion of your personal data.

Upon withdrawal of consent, your personal data will be deleted, unless it has already been permanently anonymized and included in scientific publications where you cannot be identified.
